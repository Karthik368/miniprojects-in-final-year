APJ Abdul kalam

Early Life and Education:
Dr. Kalam was born into a humble family and faced numerous challenges in his early life. Despite these obstacles, his determination and hard work led him to excel in academics. He graduated in Physics from St. Joseph’s College, Tiruchirappalli, and later pursued aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology.
Scientific Contributions:
He played a pivotal role in developing India’s first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in 1980.
He Known as the “Missile Man,” he was instrumental in the development of ballistic missiles like Agni and Prithvi.
Dr. Kalam was a key figure in India’s nuclear tests in 1998, which established India as a nuclear power.
Presidency and Public Service:
In 2002, Dr. Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India. His tenure was marked by his accessibility to the common people and his efforts to inspire the youth. He was a president who truly connected with the masses, earning the affectionate title of the “People’s President.”
Legacy and Inspiration:
Dr. Kalam’s legacy extends beyond his scientific achievements. He was a prolific writer and authored several books, including “Wings of Fire,” “Ignited Minds,” and “India 2020,” which continue to inspire readers worldwide. His vision for a developed India and his emphasis on education and innovation have left an indelible mark on the nation.
Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. His life and work continue to inspire millions, and his contributions to science, education, and public service remain a guiding light for future generations.
